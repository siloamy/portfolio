MySurrey Bridge.

Role

Ideation

Context

The City of Surrey sought to engage youth in an authentic way and on their terms. Through interviews and case studies of other cities, we found that people valued connections with friends and feeling acknowledged when engaging in activities or with others. These led to individuals forming their identities around a community, and becoming an influencer within it. To encourage this engagement, we proposed the City of Surrey foster local connections through a community network as a part of their online platform.

Process Analysis

Beginning with a set of assumptions we conducted interviews with those having adjacent experience, as the pandemic lockdown limited our opportunities. From these, we found people as youth simply wanted meaningful human connections, had the perception of government as an unapproachable entity, and the pattern of exposure to a community at a young age leading to long-term engagement. Examining other cities' strategies towards addressing or incorporating these observations, we synthesized three key insights.

Connecting

Sharing an activity with friends is a significant motivator towards continued commitment to a community group, as people want to feel connected.

Acknowledgement

The government does not seem approachable because of a lack of back-and-forth. If people were to feel acknowledged, they would be much more inclined to engage.

Identity

Individuals and communities are mutually dependent in that they both derive their identities from each other.

The detailed breakdown document for our research and insights (see on the right) can be found here.

We decided to explore the relationship between a physical and digital space, and how this could help individuals identify with the physical location of Surrey through an online platform. Leading us to proposing MySurrey Bridge, an online chat system—integrated as an addition to their current site—for local neighbourhoods to connect and engage with each other.

Reflection

This project has shown me that though insights are informed by well-conducted research, ultimately it is the designer’s experience that brings it forth. The COVID-19 lockdown made it difficult to find opportunities to speak and test with our target audience and we largely needed to depend on our instincts and experience.
